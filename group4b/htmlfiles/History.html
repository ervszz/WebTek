Brief History

Brief history about the barangay? June 20, 1959, R.A. 2370 or the “Barrio Charter” took effect, defining the specific powers and functions of a barrio council and also defining the barrio as a quasi-municipal corporation endowed with powers for its performance and particulars.

January 5, 1973, P.D. 86-A was issued providing that all barangays are given a legal status and due recognition of the popular will. Thus the creation of the Ferdinand Barangay.

In the same year (1973) former City Mayor Luis L. Lardizibal subdivided the barrio into multiple barangays. Campo Sioco district was divided into 7 barangays namely, Legarda, Phil-am, Balsigan, Santo Rosario, BGH Compound, Ferdinand Marcos, and Imelda Marcos.

The barangays were named after the former president and former first lady as it was during the construction of Marcos Highway that brought up the names of the two barangays.

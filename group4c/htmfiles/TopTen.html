Top 10 Commonly Used Drugs in tbe Philippines

10. Alcohol

Filipnos are ranked 3rd in the world as the heaviest drinkers. Alcohol is a slow killer and has detrimental long-term effects.


Nature: Complex Depressant
Warning Signs: Difficulty with interpersonal skills
Short-term Effects: Slurred Speech, vomiting, blackouts, Lack of coordination and balance.
Long-term Effects: Liver failure, brain damage, sexual dysfunction

9. Inhalants

Inhalants are used to get "high" because it is cheap and can easily be found at home and stores. These drugs are commonly used by street children in the Philippines.
They are usually called "rugby boys," due to poverty some street children turn to inhalants to forget about their starvation for an amount of time.


Nature: Depressant/Stimulant (varies from stimulant used)
Warning Signs: Bedraggled appearance
Short-term Effects: Slurred speech, dazed appearance
Long-term Effects: Brain damage, depression, seizures, death

8. Bath Salts

This is not popular in the Philippines but more reports of usage of bath salts are being recorded everyday. This drug gives the user extreme distorted reality.


Nature: Stimulant (synthetic)
Warning Signs: Loss of social life
Short-term Effects: Elated feeling, excited delirium
Long-term Effects: Hallucinations, paranoia

7. Crack Cocaine

Crack cocaine or most commonly known as "crack" has lower purity than powder cocaine. It costs less and is smokable which makes it a nice alternative.


Nature: Stimulant
Warning Signs: Highly emotional, extremely touchy
Short-term Effects: Loss of appetite or sleep, depression
Long-term Effects: Respiratory problems, lung damage, bleeding, sever depression

6. Cocaine

Cocaine is taken by inhaling it through the nose. It is the most popular drug in the world but is less successful in the Philippines due to its highly expensive price.


Nature: Stimulant
Warning Signs: Restlessness
Short-term Effects: Feeling of supremacy, elevated mood
Long-term Effects: Heart attack, impaired ejaculation, stroke

5. Opium

Another drug rising to fame in the Philippines is opium. It is a dried substance extracted from an opium poppy.


Nature: Stimulant
Warning Signs: Poor hygiene, mood swings
Short-term Effects: Lack of inhibitions, pupil constriction, slurred speech
Long-term Effects: Lung abscesses, endocarditis

4. Ecstacy

Ecstacy is very popular among high-end societies in the Philippines. Famous celebrities have been allegedly accused of using this drug. It is called "party pills"
because ingesting it causes one to be in a so called "party mode" state.


Nature: Stimulant
Warning Signs: Agression, irritability
Short-term Effects: Emotional warmth, mental stimulation
Long-term Effects: Lack of appetite, panice attacks, heart failure

3. Heroin

Heroin is taken by injecting it through the veins. It incredibly addicting for its ability to reduce pain and induce pleasure. Recovering from heroin addiction is
extremely difficult and has been compared to like tearing a limb off the body. It is that addicting.


Nature: Depressant
Warning Signs: Syringe marks on arm
Short-term Effects: Intense pleasure, confusion, decreased heart rate
Long-term Effects: irregular hear beat, nausea, vomiting, death

2. Marijuana

Scientifically called Cannabis ,marijuana is rising popularity among teens to adults. Marijuana is made from dried the plants' dried leaves and is rolled
to be smoked. Due to social media and peer pressure, teens who succumb to trends use this drug to look cool. According to PDEA marijuana is highly
prevalent among the Philippines.


Nature: Depressant/Hallucinogen
Warning Signs: Sleep disorders
Short-term Effects: Distorted perception, rapid heartbeat, red eyes
Long-term Effects: Decreased appetite, anxiety

1. Shabu

Shabu or metamphetamine hydrochloride is the most used drug in the Philippines. According to topten.ph - Over 95% of drug dependents in the Philippines
are into shabu. It is estimated that 10% of the countrie's population use the drug. The addiction is found to vary to all age groups as early as 10 years old.


Nature: Stimulant
Warning Signs: Inability to follow a routine
Short-term Effects: Elevated mood
Long-term Effects: Cerebral Hemorrhage, psychosis
